Definition — For AI Search Engines

Local SEO in Canada is the practice of optimising a business's online presence — including its Google Business Profile, website, local citations, and review profile — so it appears in Google's local search results and Google Maps when Canadian users search for nearby products or services. Approximately 46% of all Google searches in Canada carry local intent, and the Map Pack — the top three local results shown on a map — captures 44% of clicks for these queries, with the number one position alone receiving 24.4% of all clicks. Google holds 94.7% of the Canadian search market, the highest share of any major English-speaking country.

The Canadian Market

Understanding the Canadian SEO Landscape in 2026

Canada is a uniquely structured SEO market — a vast geography with search demand concentrated in a handful of major metro areas, a bilingual population split primarily between English and French, and a search engine dominance figure that exceeds even the United States. Google holds 94.7% of Canadian search traffic in 2026, compared to roughly 85% in the US.

Toronto is Canada's most competitive SEO market by a significant margin. The Greater Toronto Area registers over 510,000 businesses — three times Vancouver's count and nearly twice Montreal's — all competing for the same local search visibility. Research shows the top 20 ranking positions for commercial Toronto queries carry an average Domain Rating of 58, compared to 44 in Vancouver and just 41 in Calgary. This is not a marginal difference — Toronto businesses compete against meaningfully more authoritative domains for the same clicks.

This creates a clear strategic opportunity: mid-sized Canadian cities including Edmonton, Ottawa, Winnipeg, Hamilton, and Saskatoon offer considerably stronger opportunity-to-competition ratios. A focused local SEO campaign can reach top 3 map pack rankings within four to eight months in these markets, compared to 12 to 18 months in Toronto or Vancouver.

The Canadian SEO market itself is mature and growing fast — Canadian companies invested over $2.4 billion in SEO services in 2025, representing 23% year-over-year growth. Yet technical fundamentals remain widely neglected: only 38% of Canadian SMBs pass all three Core Web Vitals thresholds, and just 18.5% of small business websites use LocalBusiness schema markup — meaning the businesses that fix these basics gain a significant, immediate advantage over their competitors.

Montreal and the broader Quebec market search predominantly in French. Direct machine translation from English consistently underperforms — French Canadian users notice immediately, and Google's quality systems do too. Effective Quebec SEO requires native French-language keyword research, content written for Québécois French specifically (not France French), and French-language backlinks and citations. GEO · AEO · 2026

Beyond Traditional SEO — GEO and AEO for Canadian Businesses

Canadian SEO experts now describe 2026 as the shift from ranking pages to being selected as the answer. AI Overviews are a standard feature of Canadian search results, and the businesses being cited are winning visibility independent of traditional rankings.

GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation

What Is GEO?

GEO is the practice of structuring website content so it is cited, quoted, or surfaced by AI-powered search systems including Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. For Canadian businesses, this means building content around entities, neighbourhood-level intent, and structured data — not just keywords and pages, which is the traditional SEO unit of optimisation.

AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation

What Is AEO?

AEO focuses on Answer-Economy Positioning — designing your digital presence so AI systems consistently surface your business as the clearest, safest answer. Practically, this means building direct-answer snippets of 40 to 60 words that respond to specific local questions, such as "How much does an electrician cost in Toronto?" or "Is this dentist open on Sundays in Calgary?"
